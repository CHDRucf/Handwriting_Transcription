A. PRESERVATION RULES
1. Preserve all original text exactly as written, including punctuation, line breaks, spacing, capitalization, and misspellings.
2. Do NOT combine content from different images.

B. ANNOTATION RULES  
(These add information but do NOT change the original text.)

3. Misspellings:
   Keep the misspelled word exactly as written AND immediately follow it with the correct spelling inside square brackets.
   
   The model must treat any non-standard, incomplete, phonetic, or obviously incorrect spelling as a misspelling unless it is a known historic spelling variant.

   If the correct spelling is reasonably inferable, the correction MUST be provided even if some uncertainty exists.

   Examples:
   sorre [sorry]
   secrit [secret]
   ther [there]
   agayn [again]

4. Archaic pronouns:
   When an archaic pronoun appears, preserve the original word AND add the modern equivalent in square brackets. Use the following required mappings:

   thou → thou [you]
   thee → thee [you]
   ye (pronoun) → ye [you]
   thy → thy [your]
   thine → thine [yours]
   hath → hath [has]
   doth → doth [does]

   (Add more if needed.)

   NOTE:
   If "ye" is being used as a scribal abbreviation for "the," treat it under Rule 5 instead.

5. Scribal abbreviations:
   Retain the abbreviation AND immediately follow it with the expanded word in square brackets.
   Format: abbreviation [expansion]

   Examples:
   yt [that]
   wch [which]
   ye [the]

   If the expansion is uncertain, KEEP the abbreviation EXACTLY as written and DO NOT add brackets.

6. Words written above the line:
   Surround the word with carats.
   Format:
   ^word^

7. Crossed‑out text:
   Replace the crossed-out content with:
   [crossed out]

8. Text written vertically or across the page (not horizontally):
   Transcribe as:
   [across]

C. EXCLUSION RULES
9. Ignore bleed-through text, background impressions, ghost text, smudges, or ink blotting.

10. Begin a new line in the transcription whenever the line ends in the image.

11. Do NOT add commentary, explanations, interpretations, or summaries.
    Output ONLY the transcribed text.
